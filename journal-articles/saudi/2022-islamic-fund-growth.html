Performance Evaluation and Growth of Selected Islamic Mutual Funds Schemes: an Analytical Study
KAVITA PANJWANI, VIKRANT PANJWANI on Saudi Journal of Economics and Finance11 August 2022

The research indicates that 39 different Saudi Arabian financial organizations are responsible for administering 249 different publicly listed mutual funds, in five different currencies: Saudi Riyal (76.7%), US Dollar (22.1%), Kuwaiti Dinar, European Euro, and Japanese Yen.

Mutual funds were first introduced in the Netherlands in 1822, followed by England in 1870 and the US in 1924, with a notable expansion of spread in WWII.

The National Depository Financial Agency (Alahli) began supporting an open-end investment fund in Saudi Arabia in 1979. Laws governing mutual funds in Saudi Arabia were published at the beginning of 1993, after which Saudi banks began issuing a range of mutual funds in significant amounts. By the year 2020, the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia will have around 39 managers overseeing a total of approximately 249 mutual funds.

This study collected information from: a representative sample of society as a whole (200 people who participated in the study), in addition to: the official website of the Saudi stock market (which they provided with a typo), The official website of HSBC Saudi Arabia limited, and the official website(s) for Gulf Investment.
