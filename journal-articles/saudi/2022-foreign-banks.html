The Determinants Influencing Foreign Banks' Entry into Saudi Arabia
NAJLA ALOMAR, MILIND SATHYE, PETER GRAHAM on Saudi Journal of Economics and Finance24 September 2022

A five-point Likert scale questionnaire based on the five categories of foreign bank entry determinants found by previous international research was distributed to 92 CEOs, COOs, and other senior managers of all 14 foreign banks operating in Saudi Arabia in December 2019, with a 77% [overall(?)] response rate.

The DFAA analysis found that 11 determinants emerged as very important factors influencing the foreign banks' decision to enter Saudi Arabia:

  • following corporate customers: This finding does not support the literature that shows that following corporate customers is important only in developed countries
  • the size of the Saudi banking market
  • the Saudi economic and political stability
  • Saudi economic growth
  • building a new customer base: this is in line with other research
  • bank's reputation
  • level of demand for banking products and services
  • geographical links
  • entry regulations
  • the Riyal pegged to the US dollar

Determinants considered average importance were the bank's experience, bank's size, and interest rate differences. The first two reflect the parent bank characteristics and ownership advantage (eclectic paradigm). The current study results show that ownership advantages that can be used to gain a competitive advantage over local competition are not crucial factors in foreign banks' entry decisions, even though most of the foreign banks that entered Saudi Arabia are among the largest 100 banks according to Brand Finance Banking report 2019. (For example: ICBC, Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group, JP Morgan Chase and BNP Paribas, Deuts che Bank, Qatar National Bank First Abu Dhabi Bank, and Emirates NBD) Others (Bank Muscat and the National Bank of Bahrain (NBB)) are among the largest ones in their home countries.

Significantly less important were: following non - corporate customers, tax rate consideration, regulations allowing other non - banking activities, prov iding Islamic banking products in the Saudi market, prior experience of providing Islamic products outside the Saudi market, following the competitors, the legal system similarity, language similarity, and applying Islamic principles.

Foreign banks started operating in the Saudi banking market in 2000 when the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) approved the first foreign bank license in October of that year, and by 2005 only five foreign banks were operating in the country. By 2022, there are 21 licensed foreign banks in the KSA compared to only 11 domestic banks.

  • The Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) approved the first foreign bank license in October 2000.
  • By 2005, five foreign banks were operating in the country, with a market share of 31.2%.
  • In 2022, there are 21 licensed foreign banks with a market share of 65.6%, compared to 11 domestic banks.
