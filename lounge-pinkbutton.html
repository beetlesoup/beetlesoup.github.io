🦠 2022-08-05: Why stinky sweat is good for you - Michealeen Doucleff for NPR's All Things Considered
Staphylococcus hominins, one of the 200 or so bacteria on some people's skin, produces an onion smell after eating the sweat it finds on the body. It also kills MRSA(!!!) by punching holes in its cell membrane. As the water evaporates, sweat antibiotics increase in concentration. So it kind of leaves a little coating on your skin. It's antibiotic juice-turned-lotion. Don't worry about washing it off; it's deep in your pores.
