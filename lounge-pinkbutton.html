2022-08-05 NPR: Why stinky sweat is good for you - Michealeen Doucleff for All Things Considered
As you may know, underarm and genital sweat contains a cornucopia of compounds, including oils, fats, proteins, and even antimicrobial compounds that interact with the 200 or so different bacteria that live on human skin. And when they say interacting, Michaeleen Doucleff specifically means that the bacteria enjoy munching on your sweat compounds, yum yum, which results in excreting new chemical compounds.

One bacterium -and the article avoids needing to say “bacterium” by calling it a critter- called Staphylococcus hominins produces an onion or cheesey onion smell. Does everyone have it? This study found that S. hominins protects the skin from eczema (so they put it in an eczema-treating cream) and even… the big bad MRSA.

S. hominins kills MRSA by punching holes in its cell membrane. And as the water evaporates, sweat antibiotics increase in concentration. So it kind of leaves a little coating on your skin. It's antibiotic juice-turned-lotion. Don't worry about not washing it off; it's deep in your pores.
