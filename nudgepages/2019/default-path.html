Going along with the default does not mean going on with it: attrition in a charitable giving experiment
ALEXIA GAUDEUL, MAGDALENA C. KACZMAREK 09 July 2019

Along with providing evidence from various follow-up studies, this paper tests the actual donations of those who have pledged to donate as coerced by the default choice.

Conclusion:

Defaults often put people on the 'right' path, but does not necessarily motivate them to go on with further steps. Indeed, the default does little to help them understand the benefit of doing so, which greatly reduces the impact of the default.
