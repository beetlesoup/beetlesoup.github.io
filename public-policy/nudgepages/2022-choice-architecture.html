The effectiveness of nudging: A meta-analysis of choice architecture interventions across behavioral domains
STEPHANIE MERTENS, MARIO HERBERZ, ULF JJ HAHNEL, TOBIAS BROSCHCorrected 04 May 2022

How effective are choice architecture interventions overall? This study claims to have performed an exhaustive study of more than 200 published and unpublished studies

Results:

  • The majority of choice architecture interventions will successfully promote the desired behavior change with a small to large effect size, with ~15% of interventions being likely to backfire.
  • Interventions in the decision structure category (defaults, reducing effort) had a stronger effect on behavior compared to interventions in the decision information (translation, visibility) and the decision assistance (reminders, commitments) categories.
  • Interventions had a particularly strong effect on behavior in the food domain, and the smallest effects were observed in the financial domain. (Domains: health, food, environment, finance, pro-social, other)
  • Neither location (US vs non-US) nor target population (adults vs younger) had a statistically significant impact on the effect size of interventions, but more recent publications reported smaller effect sizes than older populations.
