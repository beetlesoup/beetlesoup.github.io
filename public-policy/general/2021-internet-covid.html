Does providing free internet access to low-income households affect COVID-19 spread? (Link)
DANIEL GOETZ on Health Economics11 September 2022

Yes. Having in-home WiFi reduces the propensity of residents to visit businesses in the arts, entertainment, and recreation category.

Using data from a pilot program in Toronto, we find that free WiFi extended to four large clusters of low-income apartment blocks led to a 14.4% reduction in weekly cases in that neighborhood, compared to control neighborhoods selected based on a propensity score approach using demographic and housing stock variables of neighborhoods as input features.

  • Lagged cases and vaccinations were allowed to affect current cases.
  • Because Toronto comprises a single geographic decision-making unit for COVID-19 policy, all neighborhoods are subject to the same COVID-19 government regulations at the same times.
  • Reductions in cases were concentrated amongst individuals younger than 60.
